### 00:38 — When should I replace my AC unit in Florida?
Here in Florida, you should replace your AC unit when it hits 10 to 15 years old, when it still uses old R-22 refrigerant, or when a repair would cost more than half the price of a new system. Watch for three red flags: rising electric bills, frequent breakdowns, and rooms that just won't get cold. Our heat runs these units hard year-round, so they age faster than up north. If you're seeing those signs, start planning before peak summer.

### 02:15 — How much does it cost to replace an AC unit in Fort Lauderdale?
A full AC unit replacement in Fort Lauderdale typically runs between $5,500 and $12,000 installed. The big drivers are system size — measured in tons — your SEER2 efficiency rating, and the shape of your existing ductwork. For an average single-family home, a 3-ton changeout usually lands around $7,000 to $9,500. And that should include the Broward County permit, a new line set, refrigerant, and hauling away your old unit. If a quote leaves those out, ask why.

### 04:05 — How much does a new AC system cost in Broward County?
For a complete new AC system across Broward County, plan on $6,000 to $12,000 and up, installed. The higher end is your variable-speed, high-efficiency systems — they cost more today but cut your summer power bills significantly, which adds up fast in our climate. A good contractor sizes the system with a Manual J load calculation, not a guess, so you're not paying for more unit than your home needs.

### 06:00 — How long does an AC installation take in Fort Lauderdale?
A standard AC installation in Fort Lauderdale takes about 4 to 8 hours — most changeouts are done in a single day. If we're also replacing ductwork, moving an air handler, or upgrading to a high-efficiency system, it can take the full day. We schedule fast, often same-week, so you're not sweating it out waiting.

### 07:20 — Is it better to repair or replace my AC unit?
Simple rule: if your unit is under 10 years old and it's a minor repair, fix it. But if it's past 12 years, running R-22, or the repair costs more than half a new system, replacement wins on long-term cost and reliability. We always give homeowners both numbers in writing, so you decide — no pressure.
