**Article body (draft):**

South Florida runs its air conditioners harder than almost anywhere in the country. Between year-round heat, heavy humidity, and coastal salt air, a Fort Lauderdale AC system simply doesn't last as long as one in a milder climate — which is why so many Broward County homeowners find themselves weighing the same question every summer: repair the old unit again, or replace it?

Here's how local homeowners can think it through.

**When replacement makes sense.** As a general rule, it's time to replace your AC unit in Florida when it reaches 10 to 15 years old, still uses phased-out R-22 refrigerant, or needs a repair costing more than half the price of a new system. Three warning signs tend to show up first: climbing electric bills, repeat breakdowns during peak summer, and rooms that never quite reach the thermostat setting.

**What a new system costs here.** A full AC unit replacement in Fort Lauderdale typically runs $5,500 to $12,000 installed. The price depends on system size (measured in tons), the SEER2 efficiency rating, and the condition of your existing ductwork. For an average single-family home, a 3-ton changeout usually lands around $7,000 to $9,500 — and a complete, properly scoped quote should include the Broward County permit, a new line set, refrigerant, startup testing, and removal of the old unit.

**Across Broward County**, complete new systems generally fall in the $6,000 to $12,000-plus range, with high-efficiency variable-speed units at the top — those cost more up front but meaningfully cut summer power bills.

**A few tips before you sign.** Insist on a Manual J load calculation rather than a "same size as the old one" guess, get written pricing, and ask whether financing or manufacturer rebates are available. Most reputable local installers can complete a standard changeout in 4 to 8 hours, often within the same week.
