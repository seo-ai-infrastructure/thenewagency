**Title:** How often should you actually service your AC down here? (Florida ≠ up north)

**Body:**
Quick PSA for neighbors heading into the hot months. A lot of people move to Fort Lauderdale and keep the "service the AC once a year" habit from up north — and then it quits on the first 95° day.

Down here our systems run almost year-round, and the humidity + salt air are rough on coils and drain lines. The general rule is **twice a year** — spring and fall. The single most common thing we see fail in this neighborhood is a clogged condensate drain line (humidity), which can overflow into a closet or ceiling. That's a cheap fix if it's caught early during a tune-up.

A standard tune-up around here runs roughly $79–$149 and should include coil cleaning, refrigerant + electrical checks, capacitor test, and clearing that drain line.

**Headline:** Why Fort Lauderdale Air Conditioners Need Twice-a-Year Maintenance (and What a Real Tune-Up Includes)

**Author:** [Owner/Technician Name], House AC Repair — Licensed Florida HVAC Contractor

**Article:**

Most homeowners inherit their AC-maintenance habits from wherever they lived before. If that was anywhere north of Orlando, the habit is usually "service it once a year." In Fort Lauderdale and across Broward County, that schedule quietly sets you up for a mid-summer failure.

**The Florida difference**
A central AC in the Northeast might run four months a year. A system here runs closer to eleven. Add year-round humidity and coastal salt air, and the wear profile is completely different: pitted outdoor coils, corroded contactors, and condensate drain lines that clog with biological growth. That's why the working standard in South Florida is **two professional services a year** — one in spring before peak load, one in fall.

**What a legitimate AC tune-up includes**
A real tune-up follows a checklist, not a stopwatch. At minimum it should cover: condenser and evaporator coil cleaning; refrigerant pressure and superheat/subcooling checks; capacitor, contactor, and electrical-connection testing; condensate drain clearing and treatment; filter and airflow inspection; and thermostat calibration with a measured temperature split. The homeowner should walk away with a written report.

**The economics**
A single tune-up in the Fort Lauderdale market runs about $79–$149. The bigger value is what it prevents: dirty coils and weak capacitors are behind a large share of the emergency calls we run every August. Documented maintenance also keeps manufacturer warranties valid and measurably lowers energy use — which matters when your system is the single biggest line on an FPL bill.

**The takeaway for South Florida homeowners**
Service twice a year, insist on a real checklist, and don't ignore that drain line. Whether you call us or someone else, the cadence is what protects you.
