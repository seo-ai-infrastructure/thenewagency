## How often should you service your AC in Florida?

In Florida you should service your AC **twice a year** — once in spring before the cooling season peaks and once in fall — because units here run nearly year-round and endure high humidity and salt air. This is double the once-a-year northern cadence and keeps efficiency high while preventing summer breakdowns. (Keyword: *how often should you service your ac in florida*.)

### Why twice a year in Fort Lauderdale specifically

A Minnesota AC might run 4 months a year; a Fort Lauderdale AC runs closer to 11. That's nearly 3x the runtime, plus humidity that breeds mold in drain lines and salt air that pits outdoor coils. Spring service preps you for the brutal May–October stretch; fall service catches the wear that summer caused. Homes near the beach (Las Olas, Victoria Park, Lauderdale-by-the-Sea) often benefit from a third coil rinse because of salt exposure.

## What does an AC tune-up include?

A professional **AC tune-up** includes cleaning the condenser and evaporator coils, checking refrigerant charge and pressures, testing the capacitor and electrical connections, clearing and treating the condensate drain line, inspecting the blower and filter, and verifying thermostat accuracy and temperature split. You receive a written report of findings. (Keyword: *ac tune up fort lauderdale*.)

### Our 20-point Fort Lauderdale tune-up checklist

- Rinse and clean condenser coil (salt-air buildup)
- Inspect and clean evaporator coil
- Check refrigerant pressures and superheat/subcooling
- Test capacitor, contactor, and electrical connections
- Clear and treat the condensate drain line (humidity = clogs)
- Inspect/replace the air filter and check airflow
- Lubricate motors and inspect the blower wheel
- Verify thermostat calibration and the cooling temperature split
- Tighten fasteners and inspect the disconnect and breaker
