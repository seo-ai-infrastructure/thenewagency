### 0:35 — How often should you service your AC in Florida?
"Up north, once a year is fine. Here in Florida? You want to service your AC **twice a year** — once in spring before peak cooling season, and once in the fall. The reason is simple: our systems run nearly year-round, and humidity plus salt air do a number on coils, drain lines, and electrical parts. If you're right on the water — Las Olas, Lauderdale-by-the-Sea — a third coil rinse a year isn't a bad idea. Bottom line: twice a year is the Florida standard."

### 2:10 — What does an AC tune-up include?
"A real tune-up isn't just a glance and a sticker. Here's our 20-point checklist: we clean the condenser and evaporator coils, check refrigerant pressures, test the capacitor and electrical connections, clear and treat that condensate drain line — which clogs constantly in our humidity — inspect the blower and filter, and verify the thermostat and temperature split. You get a written report and before-and-after photos. If a tech is in and out in ten minutes, that's not a tune-up."

### 4:05 — How much does an AC tune-up cost near me?
"In the Fort Lauderdale and Broward County area, a standard tune-up runs about **$79 to $149**. The range depends on your system and whether it's a one-time visit or part of a maintenance plan. Plan members pay less per visit and get priority scheduling plus a discount on repairs. We give you flat, upfront pricing before we touch anything — no surprises on the invoice."

### 5:40 — What is included in AC maintenance service in Broward County?
"Maintenance service is the recurring version — two scheduled visits a year that combine the full tune-up with proactive part inspection, a refrigerant top-off if it's low, drain-line treatment, and efficiency tuning. The payoff: fewer breakdowns, lower power bills, longer equipment life, and it keeps your manufacturer warranty valid, since most require documented annual service."

### 7:15 — Signs your AC needs a tune-up
"Don't wait for a total failure. Warm or weak airflow, a power bill creeping up, water around the air handler, musty smells, short cycling, or new noises — those are all signs. The number one humidity-driven issue we see in Fort Lauderdale is a clogged condensate drain, and that's a five-minute fix during a tune-up before it floods your closet or ceiling."

### 8:30 — One-time tune-up vs. maintenance plan
"If you just moved in or skipped a season, a one-time tune-up is perfect. But since Florida systems need service twice a year anyway, most homeowners save money with the plan — two visits, priority emergency service, and repair discounts. It usually pays for itself within a season."
