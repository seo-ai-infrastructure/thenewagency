How often should you service your AC in Florida?

In Florida you should service your AC twice a year — once in spring before the cooling season peaks and once in fall — because units here run nearly year-round and endure high humidity and salt air. This is double the once-a-year northern cadence and keeps efficiency high while preventing summer breakdowns.

What does an AC tune-up include?

A professional AC tune-up includes cleaning the condenser and evaporator coils, checking refrigerant charge and pressures, testing the capacitor and electrical connections, clearing and treating the condensate drain line, inspecting the blower and filter, and verifying thermostat accuracy and temperature split. You receive a written report of findings.

  • Rinse and clean condenser coil (salt-air buildup)
  • Check refrigerant pressures and superheat/subcooling
  • Test capacitor, contactor, and electrical connections
  • Clear and treat the condensate drain line (humidity = clogs)
  • Inspect/replace air filter and verify thermostat calibration
