How much does AC repair cost in Florida overall?

Across Florida, AC repair averages $200–$700, slightly higher than the U.S. average because year-round cooling means harder-working systems and more refrigerant and compressor calls. Coastal Broward County homes also face salt-air corrosion that accelerates coil and contactor wear.

How much does an AC refrigerant recharge cost in Fort Lauderdale?

An AC refrigerant recharge in Fort Lauderdale costs $250 to $650, depending on refrigerant type and how much your system lost. R-410A systems are cheaper to recharge than older R-22 units. A low charge means a leak — a proper repair finds and seals the leak first.

What is the average cost to fix an AC not blowing cold air?

The average cost to fix an AC not blowing cold air in Fort Lauderdale is $150 to $650. Common causes: a bad capacitor ($150–$300), low refrigerant from a leak ($250–$650), a dirty or frozen evaporator coil ($200–$500), or a failed contactor ($150–$350).

Is it worth repairing an AC unit or should you replace it?

Repairing is usually worth it when your AC is under 10 years old and the fix costs less than $500. Use the “$5,000 rule”: multiply the repair cost by the unit's age — if it exceeds $5,000, lean toward replacement.
