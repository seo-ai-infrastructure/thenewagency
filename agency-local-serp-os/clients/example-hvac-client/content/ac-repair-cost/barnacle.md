## 1. PATCH.com — Local Article (primary barnacle)

**Platform:** Patch.com (Fort Lauderdale, FL)
**Suggested section:** Neighbor Posts / Local Voices — Home & Garden
**Headline:** How Much Does AC Repair Cost in Fort Lauderdale in 2025? A Local Tech Breaks It Down

**Body:**

If you live in Fort Lauderdale, you already know your air conditioner is the hardest-working appliance in your home. So when it fails, the first question is always the same: *what is this going to cost me?* Here are honest, current price ranges for AC repair across Broward County — no sales pitch, just numbers.

**The short answer:** most AC repairs in Fort Lauderdale cost between **$150 and $650**, with the average around **$300**. The biggest swing is whether you need a small electrical part or a major component like a compressor.

**Common Fort Lauderdale AC repair costs (2025):**

- Diagnostic / service call: **$79–$129** (good companies credit this toward the repair)
- Capacitor replacement: **$150–$300** — the single most common AC failure here
- Contactor replacement: **$150–$350** — our coastal salt air corrodes these fast
- Refrigerant recharge (R-410A): **$250–$500**
- Refrigerant recharge (older R-22): **$400–$650+**
- Blower motor: **$300–$600**
- Evaporator coil cleaning/repair: **$200–$500**
- Compressor (parts + labor): **$1,200–$2,400**

**Why South Florida costs a little more:** our systems run nearly year-round, and coastal humidity and salt air wear out electrical contacts and coils faster than in cooler states. Homes still running phased-out R-22 refrigerant face the steepest recharge bills.

**One money-saving tip most people don't know:** if a tech says you're "low on refrigerant," that means you have a *leak*. Topping it off without sealing the leak just means you'll be low again in weeks. Always pay to fix the leak, not just refill.

**Repair or replace?** A good rule of thumb is the "$5,000 rule": multiply the repair cost by your unit's age. If it tops $5,000 — say, a $1,800 compressor on a 14-year-old unit — replacement usually makes more sense in our climate.
