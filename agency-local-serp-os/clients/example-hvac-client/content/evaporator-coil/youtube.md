### 0:38 — How much does evaporator coil repair cost in Fort Lauderdale?
"Evaporator coil repair in Fort Lauderdale usually runs $300 to $700. That covers leak detection, sealing a small accessible leak, and recharging refrigerant. If your system still uses older R-22 refrigerant, expect the high end or more — R-22 is discontinued and expensive. Repair only makes sense on newer coils with a small, reachable leak."

### 2:05 — How much does it cost to replace an AC evaporator coil?
"A full evaporator coil replacement in Fort Lauderdale runs about $900 to $2,800 — coil, refrigerant, labor, and disposal included. The good news: if your coil is still under the manufacturer's parts warranty, you often pay labor only, which keeps many jobs in the $600 to $1,200 range. Always ask the tech to check your warranty first."

### 3:40 — Can a leaking evaporator coil be repaired, or should it be replaced?
"Sometimes. A small, reachable leak on a newer coil can be repaired. But here's the catch: coils that leak from corrosion — which is most of what we see in humid, salt-air Broward County — usually have multiple weak spots. Patch one and another shows up. My rule: if the coil is out of warranty or older than about 8 years, replace it instead of chasing leaks."

### 5:10 — What are the signs your AC evaporator coil is bad?
"Watch for these signs: warm air from the vents, ice or frost on the indoor coil or the refrigerant line, a hissing or bubbling sound, a faint sweet chemical smell, cooling cycles that run way longer, water pooling near the air handler, and rising power bills. If you see ice forming, shut the system off — running it low on refrigerant can wreck your compressor."

"Replace just the coil when your condenser and compressor are healthy and under 8 to 10 years old.
