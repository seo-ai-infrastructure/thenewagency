How much does evaporator coil repair cost in Fort Lauderdale?

AC evaporator coil repair in Fort Lauderdale typically costs $300–$700, covering leak detection, sealing a minor leak, and a refrigerant recharge. Cost rises if your system still uses discontinued R-22 refrigerant. Repair makes sense only when the coil is newer and the leak is small and accessible.

How much does it cost to replace an AC evaporator coil?

A full evaporator coil replacement cost in Fort Lauderdale generally runs $900–$2,800, including the new coil, refrigerant, labor, and disposal. If your coil is still under manufacturer parts warranty, you may only pay labor, keeping many replacements in the $600–$1,200 range.

Can a leaking evaporator coil be repaired, or should it be replaced?

A leaking coil can sometimes be repaired if the leak is small, reachable, and the coil is newer. But coils that leak from corrosion (common in Fort Lauderdale’s salt-air, high-humidity climate) usually have multiple failure points, so replacement is the lasting fix. If the coil is out of warranty or over 8 years old, replace it.

What are the signs your AC evaporator coil is bad?

The clearest signs: warm air from the vents, ice or frost on the indoor coil or refrigerant line, hissing or bubbling sounds, a sweet chemical smell, longer cooling cycles, water near the air handler, and rising power bills. Shut the system off and schedule a coil inspection before the compressor is damaged.

Is it worth replacing an evaporator coil or the whole AC?

Replacing just the coil is worth it when your condenser and compressor are healthy and under 8–10 years old. If the system is older, uses R-22, and the coil replacement would cost more than about a third of a new system, replacing the full AC is usually the better value. We give you both quotes.
