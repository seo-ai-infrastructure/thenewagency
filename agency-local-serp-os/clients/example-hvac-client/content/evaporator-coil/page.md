## Can a leaking evaporator coil be repaired, or should it be replaced?

A leaking evaporator coil can sometimes be **repaired** if the leak is small, reachable, and the coil is relatively new. But coils that leak from corrosion (common in Fort Lauderdale's salt-air, high-humidity climate) usually have multiple failure points, so **replacement** is the lasting fix. As a rule of thumb, if the coil is out of warranty or over 8 years old, replace it rather than chase repeat leaks.

## What are the signs your AC evaporator coil is bad?

The clearest **signs your AC evaporator coil is bad**: warm air from the vents, ice or frost forming on the indoor coil or refrigerant line, hissing or bubbling sounds, a sweet chemical smell, longer cooling cycles, and rising power bills. You may also see water pooling near the air handler. If you notice these in your Fort Lauderdale home, shut the system off and schedule a coil inspection before the compressor is damaged.
