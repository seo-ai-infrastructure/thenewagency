## PLATFORM 1 — Patch.com Article (primary barnacle)

**Suggested Patch section:** Fort Lauderdale, FL — Home & Garden / Neighbor Post
**Headline:** Evaporator Coil Repair vs. Replacement Cost in Fort Lauderdale: What Homeowners Should Expect in 2026

**Body:**

If your AC is blowing warm air or icing up this summer, the culprit is often the evaporator coil — and Fort Lauderdale homeowners are right to wonder whether they're looking at a few hundred dollars or a few thousand.

Here's the short version local homeowners ask us about most:

**What an evaporator coil does.** It's the indoor part of your AC that pulls heat and humidity out of your home's air. In South Florida's salt air and near-constant humidity, these coils corrode and develop refrigerant leaks faster than in drier climates.

**Repair cost.** A coil leak repair plus refrigerant recharge in Fort Lauderdale typically runs **$300–$700**. Systems still using discontinued R-22 refrigerant cost more. Repair is worth it only on a newer coil with a small, accessible leak.

**Replacement cost.** A full evaporator coil replacement generally runs **$900–$2,800** — coil, refrigerant, labor, and disposal. If your coil is still under the manufacturer's parts warranty, you may pay labor only ($600–$1,200).

**Signs your coil is going bad.** Warm air, frost on the indoor coil or line, hissing sounds, a sweet smell, longer cycles, water near the air handler, and climbing electric bills.

**Repair or replace?** Corrosion leaks usually have multiple weak points, so if the coil is out of warranty or older than about 8 years, replacement is the lasting fix. And if the whole system is 10+ years old and running R-22, pricing out a full replacement is smart.

**The bottom line for Broward homeowners:** get an on-site diagnosis before a low-refrigerant system damages your compressor — a far costlier repair.
